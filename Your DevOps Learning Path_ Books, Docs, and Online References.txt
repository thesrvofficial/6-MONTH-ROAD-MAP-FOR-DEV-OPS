# The DevOps Toolkit: Must-Read Books and References for Success


List of essential **DevOps books and reference materials** that align with your 6-month plan. These resources cover everything from Linux, Git, Docker, Kubernetes, Terraform, Jenkins, and more.

---

### **Foundational Books (Linux, Networking, Python, DevOps Basics):**  
1. **“The Linux Command Line: A Complete Introduction” by William Shotts**  
   - A comprehensive introduction to Linux shell commands.  

2. **“How Linux Works: What Every Superuser Should Know” by Brian Ward**  
   - Deep dive into Linux internals, great for mastering Linux fundamentals.  

3. **“Python Crash Course” by Eric Matthes**  
   - A hands-on guide to Python basics and automation projects.  

4. **“Networking for System Administrators” by Michael W. Lucas**  
   - A simple, practical guide to computer networking for DevOps engineers.  

---

### **DevOps Culture and Practices:**  
1. **“The Phoenix Project: A Novel About IT, DevOps, and Helping Your Business Win” by Gene Kim, Kevin Behr, George Spafford**  
   - A must-read that introduces DevOps through storytelling.  

2. **“The DevOps Handbook: How to Create World-Class Agility, Reliability, & Security” by Gene Kim, Jez Humble, Patrick Debois, John Willis**  
   - A practical guide to implementing DevOps practices in real-world projects.  

3. **“Accelerate: The Science of Lean Software and DevOps” by Nicole Forsgren, Jez Humble, Gene Kim**  
   - Provides insights into DevOps performance metrics and organizational success.  

---

### **Version Control (Git and GitHub):**  
1. **“Pro Git” by Scott Chacon and Ben Straub** (Free online)  
   - The ultimate Git guide, covering everything from basics to advanced workflows.  

2. **“Git for Teams” by Emma Hogbin Westby**  
   - Focuses on collaborative Git workflows for large teams.  

---

### **Containerization and Docker:**  
1. **“Docker Deep Dive” by Nigel Poulton**  
   - One of the best books to understand Docker from scratch to advanced concepts.  

2. **“The Docker Book: Containerization is the New Virtualization” by James Turnbull**  
   - A comprehensive introduction to Docker and containerization.  

---

### **Kubernetes:**  
1. **“Kubernetes Up & Running” by Kelsey Hightower, Brendan Burns, Joe Beda**  
   - A fantastic introduction to Kubernetes with practical examples.  

2. **“The Kubernetes Book” by Nigel Poulton**  
   - Short and practical, providing hands-on Kubernetes knowledge.  

3. **“Kubernetes Patterns: Reusable Elements for Designing Cloud-Native Applications” by Bilgin Ibryam and Roland Huß**  
   - A great book for mastering Kubernetes design patterns.  

---

### **Infrastructure as Code (Terraform, Ansible):**  
1. **“Terraform: Up & Running” by Yevgeniy Brikman**  
   - The best book for learning Terraform from the ground up.  

2. **“Ansible for DevOps: Server and Configuration Management for Humans” by Jeff Geerling**  
   - A detailed book covering Ansible for infrastructure automation.  

---

### **CI/CD and Automation (Jenkins):**  
1. **“Jenkins: The Definitive Guide” by John Ferguson Smart**  
   - The go-to reference for Jenkins pipelines and automation.  

2. **“Continuous Delivery: Reliable Software Releases through Build, Test, and Deployment Automation” by Jez Humble and David Farley**  
   - Explains CI/CD best practices and strategies.  

---

### **Monitoring and Logging (EFK Stack):**  
1. **“The Art of Monitoring” by James Turnbull**  
   - Focuses on monitoring systems, including the ELK stack.  

2. **“The Logstash Book” by James Turnbull**  
   - A practical guide to log management and analysis.
